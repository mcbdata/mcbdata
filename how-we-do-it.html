What We Provide

The Approach

Operationalizing Information Governance requires a multi-dimensional approach: engaging senior management and the leadership of control, IT, and business functions; formulating an enterprise-wide operating model that considers Regulatory Intelligence, Governance, Information Governance Technology, and Data Identification and Retrieval; and establishing clear organizational capabilities and responsibilities. The result is effective Information Governance, that meets regulatory requirements, provides a strong defense in response to regulatory inquiry, and reduces risks and costs.

Close the capability gaps with an Implementation Strategy, including whether to “Build” or “Buy” and a Blueprint that details changes in Governance, People, Process, and Technology

Proprietary IG Operating Framework

  • Using specific Information Management capabilities and communications to efficiently assess IG risk and IG business objectives
  • Each IG objective is aligned with the set of Capabilities and Sub-capabilities necessary for effective operations
  • Each Capability is aligned with typical financial services organizational roles (e.g., Compliance, IT, Risk), based on our experience, allowing for efficient and appropriate inquiry

How it differs from traditional IG maturity models

  • IG-industry models describe an existing overall ‘state’
    • (e.g., “Ad Hoc, Inconsistent”), not the maturity of the TOM components that enable effective IG
  • IG-industry models pursue maturity ‘levels’
    • (e.g., “Automated and Fully Integrated Across Functions”), rather than mitigating specific critical risks or achieving specific business objectives
